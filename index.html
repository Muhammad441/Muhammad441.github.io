Muhammad Suhail Saleem

Hi! I am a Ph.D. student at the Robotics Institute at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Professor Maxim Likhachev. My thesis research, Manipulation in the Blind, focuses on enabling robot manipulators to leverage contact in vision-impaired or occluded settings, allowing them to better perceive their environment and robustly and efficiently complete tasks. You can find my thesis document here.

More broadly, I am interested in developing and deploying reasoning, motion planning, and decision-making systems that enable robots to operate reliably in uncertain and unstructured real-world scenarios. My work has spanned a range of tasks, including non-prehensile manipulation, object stacking, inspection, and projectile interception, as well as a variety of robotic embodiments such as manipulators, snake robots, ground robots, and mobile manipulators.
